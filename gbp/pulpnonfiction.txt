One night only this Saturday: Pulp Non-Fiction is a live storytelling show where comedians and everyday storytellers share true stories from their own lives, all performed in English.

Humans have been telling each other stories for millennia. This is that ancient tradition, kept alive with a microphone and a drink in hand. Expect the strange, the surreal and the very funny, as host and local comedian Albert Louw brings together storytellers from all kinds of backgrounds and countries, each with a true tale you won't hear anywhere else.

If you're visiting the city, new in town or just curious, it's an easy and welcoming night out. No Swiss German needed, no audience participation required: just settle in with a drink and listen. It's also a relaxed way to meet people if you're on your own, since storytelling crowds tend to be a chatty bunch.
Admission is free. Donations are appreciated, but what matters most is your presence. Seats are limited, so reserve your spot in advance to make sure you get in.

Pulp Non-Fiction is part of IN YOUR FACE Comedy, running English-language live shows in Zürich since 2018.
